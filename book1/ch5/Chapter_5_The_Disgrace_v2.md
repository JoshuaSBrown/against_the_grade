Against the Grade --- Book 1: Birthright
========================================

Chapter 5: The Disgrace
-----------------------

### Detailed Scene Outline

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Chapter Overview
----------------

**POV:** Baret, close third \[POV/person still open\] **Time:** The
between-Proving period. Weeks between Proving 1 and Proving 2. Candidates remain
in barracks at the keep --- no home visits. **Setting:** Pakran --- the
barracks, the keep courtyard, the training grounds **Emotional Register:**
Barracks claustrophobia → the ejection (bureaucratic destruction as spectacle) →
fourth inaction (self-loathing peaks) → Barlas as hostile presence → the cohort
under pressure

**Purpose:** The between-Proving period deepens the cohort through forced
proximity in barracks. Elowen's family is publicly ejected from the keep over an
unpayable tax demand --- bureaucratic destruction witnessed by the entire
candidate cohort. Baret's fourth inaction brings his self-loathing to its peak.
Barlas is a hostile, angry presence nobody wants near them --- not yet a
companion, not yet anything but trouble. The chapter is the pressure chamber.
Chapter 6 is the explosion.

**Establishes:** The barracks as the cohort's world (no escape, no home visits).
Elowen's family disgrace as bureaucratic violence --- technically legal,
politically targeted. The ejection as a public spectacle in the keep courtyard,
witnessed by candidates. Barlas as angry and isolated --- not liked by anyone
including Baret. The fourth inaction as the one that fills the ledger. Maia's
absence (she can't reach Baret in barracks, amplifying his isolation).

**Escalation from Chapter 4:** Ch 4 ended with the twin hierarchy
triple-confirmed and Haldrin noting Baret from above. Ch 5 shows the candidates
trapped inside the system --- barracks life, no escape to village or family. The
ejection demonstrates what happens to people who fall behind in Haldrin's
economy. The system doesn't need violence to destroy you. It just needs a number
you can't pay.

### Reader Hooks

**Pressure hook:** Four inactions. The reader can feel the breaking point
approaching. Each silence has been heavier than the last.

**Political hook:** The ejection is political chess. Haldrin revoking
traditional exemptions and demanding back-payment is a weapon aimed at Murek ---
destroying Murek's friend to warn Murek himself. Elowen's family is collateral.

**Barlas hook (inverted):** Instead of the reader warming to Barlas, the reader
should be wary of him. The angry kid nobody likes. The one who picks fights,
eats alone, radiates hostility. Something is wrong with him and nobody wants to
find out what.

**Forward hook:** Proving 2 is coming. After the Proving, candidates get town
privileges. Baret will encounter Elowen outside the keep --- somewhere she's now
vulnerable.

### Scene 1: Barracks Life (Early between-Proving period)

**Value shift:** Proving 1 ends → Barracks reality (trapped inside the system) →
Cohort dynamics under forced proximity

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Element                          Description
  -------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------
  Inciting Incident                Proving 1 concludes. The candidates don't go
                                   home. They're placed in barracks at the keep
                                   --- Haldrin's fortress. Training continues
                                   between Provings. The trial year is immersive.

  Progressive Complication         Barracks life reveals the cohort under
                                   pressure. Forty to sixty teenagers in close
                                   quarters, ranked, competing, forming alliances
                                   and enmities. No escape to village or family.
                                   The keep walls are the world.

  Crisis                           Value-loading beat. The claustrophobia and
                                   hierarchy settling in.

  Climax                           The barracks as a social world: who sleeps
                                   where, who eats with whom, who's rising and
                                   who's sinking after Proving 1 results.

  Resolution                       The cohort has a geography now. And Baret is
                                   increasingly visible within it --- his strong
                                   marks and Proving 1 performance put him on the
                                   map.
  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Scene Detail:**

**The barracks.** Inside the keep. Communal quarters --- rows of bunks or
sleeping pallets, shared meals, shared training grounds. The candidates live
where the lord's apparatus can watch them. Privacy is minimal. The hierarchy
from the Marking and Proving 1 is already structuring how the cohort organizes
itself --- strong-marked candidates claiming better positions, collaborator
children clustering, original-family kids finding each other.

**No home visits.** This matters. Baret can't see Maia. Can't return to Tanapun,
can't hear her voice, can't be grounded by the mountain and the sheep and the
familiar. He's cut off from everything that anchored him. Darek is here, but the
twin drift from Chapter 4's aftermath continues --- Darek gravitating toward
Kullen's social warmth, Baret more isolated in observation.

**Sera** in the barracks. Her sharp tongue is an asset and a liability in close
quarters. She makes enemies easily. She also makes people laugh, which buys her
tolerance. Through Baret's POV: Sera is a known quantity from Tanapun, someone
he's wary of since "you were closer than me." Not an ally. Not an enemy. A
presence.

**Garik** competitive and aggressive in the barracks. Jostling for position. His
resentment of Baret's strong marks may be surfacing --- the woodcutter's son
from the same village who's watching the herder get noticed.

**The training between Provings.** Continued weapons work, endurance training,
the assessors still watching. The candidates know Proving 2 is coming. The
pressure is constant.

**Maia's absence.** Through Baret's POV: a gap. He might think of her, of
Tanapun, of the warning she gave him. He can't act on it. She's a day's travel
away and the barracks don't have visiting hours. The isolation amplifies
everything --- without Maia's voice to reinforce caution, Baret's internal
pressure has no release valve.

### Scene 2: The Ejection (Mid between-Proving period)

**Value shift:** Routine keep life → Bureaucratic violence as public spectacle →
Fourth inaction → Self-loathing peaks

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Element                          Description
  -------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------
  Inciting Incident                The candidates are assembled in the keep
                                   courtyard --- possibly for training, for a
                                   formation, for some routine purpose. Then the
                                   ejection begins.

  Progressive Complication         Elowen's family is publicly removed from the
                                   keep. Her father, from an old proud house, has
                                   held traditional exemptions from certain taxes
                                   --- reduced tributes, specific privileges that
                                   predate Haldrin's lordship. These are the
                                   remnants of the old order: not wealth, not
                                   power, just the administrative recognition
                                   that this family was here before the current
                                   lord and retains certain rights. Haldrin has
                                   revoked the exemptions and demanded
                                   back-payment covering the years the exemptions
                                   were in effect. The amount is calculated to be
                                   unpayable --- the family has status but no
                                   money. Old name, no resources. When the father
                                   cannot meet the demand, the keep residence is
                                   seized as payment. The family is ejected.

  Crisis                           Baret is standing in formation with the
                                   cohort. He watches Elowen's family walk out.
                                   Does he react? There is nothing to fight. No
                                   villain beating someone. No physical cruelty.
                                   Just a family carrying bundles through a
                                   courtyard, and a system that can destroy you
                                   with arithmetic.

  Climax                           Baret does nothing. Fourth inaction. The most
                                   devastating one because the destruction is so
                                   quiet. No one is being struck. No one is
                                   screaming. A family is losing their home,
                                   their status, their place in the world, and it
                                   looks like an administrative process because
                                   it is one. The paperwork is correct. The
                                   demand is technically legal. The family simply
                                   cannot pay.

  Resolution                       The courtyard empties. The ejection is
                                   complete. The cohort returns to training. Life
                                   in the keep continues as if nothing happened.
                                   That normalcy is the worst part.
  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Scene Detail:**

**The mechanism in detail.** Elowen's father holds traditional exemptions ---
hereditary privileges that old houses have maintained for generations. Maybe
reduced taxation on specific goods, exemption from certain labor levies, the
right to keep residence at the keep. These aren't valuable enough to matter
economically. They're valuable symbolically: they represent legitimacy that
doesn't flow from Haldrin.

Haldrin revokes the exemptions --- which is within his authority as lord. Then
he demands back-payment: the taxes the family would have owed if the exemptions
had never existed, calculated across years. Maybe a decade. Maybe longer. The
amount is enormous relative to the family's resources. The family has status but
no money. This is established in Elowen's character file: "old name, no money,
pride without resources."

The father cannot pay. The keep residence --- the family's last tangible symbol
of their standing --- is seized as payment. The family is given whatever time
the system allows (probably very little) to collect their belongings and leave.

**Why now.** The timing is political. Elowen's father is friends with Murek.
Haldrin sees the relationship between an old house (traditional legitimacy) and
the Oligarch (economic power) as a potential threat. The ejection is a message
to Murek: I can destroy your allies with paperwork. The charge is real. The
timing is tactical. The target is Murek. The victim is Elowen's family.

**The public execution.** The ejection happens in the keep courtyard. Not
hidden, not private. Haldrin's officials oversee the process --- perhaps reading
the revocation, perhaps announcing the debt, perhaps simply standing by while
the family carries their belongings through the courtyard. The candidate cohort
is present --- in formation for training, or assembled deliberately so they see.

**What the candidates see.** A family walking through the courtyard with
bundles. Elowen among them --- the quiet girl from the Marking, the one Baret
registered without understanding. Her composure holds. She doesn't cry, doesn't
stumble, doesn't look at anyone. She walks with the discipline forged from her
character --- stoicism as the only armor she has left. Her father walks with the
rigid dignity of a proud man being publicly humiliated. The officials are
administrative, not cruel. They don't need to be cruel. The process does the
work.

**Elowen through Baret's POV.** He sees her. He recognizes her from the Marking
--- the girl he slid past, the background presence. She's not background now.
She's walking through the courtyard carrying what's left of her family's life.
Through Baret's structural thinking: he can read what's happening. The
exemptions revoked, the back-payment demanded, the residence seized. The
political chess underneath: Murek's friend, Haldrin's warning. Baret can see the
architecture of this destruction. He cannot stop it.

**Murek's absence.** Murek doesn't intervene. He might not even be present. His
silence is its own message: I cannot defend you without confirming the alliance
Haldrin is trying to prevent. The Oligarch calculates. His friend loses
everything. Through Baret's POV: another powerful person doing nothing. The
pattern is everywhere --- Baret's own inaction mirrored in the institutions
above him.

**Baret's fourth inaction.** He's in formation. He watches. He does nothing.
There is nothing to do --- no one is being beaten, no one is being attacked. The
system is functioning as designed. You can't punch paperwork. You can't step
between a family and an arithmetic problem. The self-loathing peaks because
Baret can see the injustice with perfect clarity and has no mechanism to address
it. His previous inactions had the excuse of physical danger --- the retainer at
the feast, the child at the Marking. This one doesn't even offer that excuse. He
simply has no power. The system works precisely because individuals can't stop
it.

**The cohort's reaction.** Some candidates look away. Some watch with the
careful blankness of people who know they could be next. Some --- the
collaborator children --- are unbothered. Kullen might be absent or might watch
with the complicated reaction of a lord's son seeing his father's power
exercised on someone he knows.

**Backstory seeds:** - The ejection as bureaucratic violence. Payoff: The system
doesn't need to hit you. It just needs a number you can't pay. This is what
Baret's republic tries to change. - Elowen's composure in the courtyard. Payoff:
Ch 6 (the teenagers target this composure). Book 4 (the token return costs her
everything the composure protects). - Murek's calculated silence. Payoff: Ch 20
(Murek speaks only when his own son is threatened). Murek is never an ally. -
The fourth inaction as different in kind. Payoff: Ch 6 breaking point. The
ledger is full not because the injustice was the worst but because Baret has run
out of capacity for self-contempt.

### Scene 3: Barracks Pressure / Barlas at the Edges (Across the between-Proving weeks)

**Value shift:** Post-ejection tension → Cohort dynamics intensifying → Barlas
as hostile, isolated, dangerous

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Element                          Description
  -------------------------------- ----------------------------------------------
  Inciting Incident                Life in the barracks continues after the
                                   ejection. The cohort is processing what they
                                   saw. Training intensifies as Proving 2
                                   approaches.

  Progressive Complication         The barracks is a pressure cooker. Candidates
                                   are forming alliances and enmities. Darek is
                                   gravitating toward Kullen's warmth. Baret is
                                   increasingly isolated in his observations and
                                   self-loathing. And Barlas is a problem.

  Crisis                           Value-loading beat. The cohort's social
                                   architecture is solidifying.

  Climax                           The barracks world before Proving 2: who's
                                   aligned with whom, who's dangerous, who's
                                   vulnerable. The pressure is building toward
                                   something.

  Resolution                       Proving 2 is announced. The candidates will be
                                   paired and sent into the field. Baret doesn't
                                   know yet that the pairing is a weapon.
  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Scene Detail:**

**Barlas as hostile and isolated.** This is the critical change from the
previous outline. Barlas is NOT becoming Baret's companion during this period.
Barlas is the angry kid nobody wants near them. Specific behaviors:

-   He picks fights during training. Not competitive sparring --- genuine
    aggression that the assessors have to break up. He fights like someone who
    wants to hurt people, not like someone who wants to win.
-   He eats alone. Not by choice --- by the social gravity of hostility. People
    have tried sitting with him. It went badly. He radiates "don't come near me"
    with physical intensity.
-   His anger is disproportionate and unpredictable. A comment that would be
    banter between other candidates triggers a reaction from Barlas that's
    frightening. He goes from calm to violent faster than anyone expects.
-   When Haldrin's name comes up, or the system's authority is visible, Barlas
    gets quieter --- but it's not the quiet of acceptance. It's the quiet of
    something pressurized. The other candidates can feel it. They stay away.

**Through Baret's POV:** Barlas is not a kindred spirit. Not yet. Baret reads
Barlas as dangerous, unstable, someone to avoid. The structural thinker in Baret
might register that Barlas's anger has a source --- it's too focused, too
specific, to be random. But he doesn't pursue the observation. He has his own
problems. Barlas is someone else's problem.

**What the reader sees that Baret doesn't:** The undertone. When the ejection
happens, Barlas's reaction --- if visible --- is different from the other
candidates'. Not performative sympathy. Something personal, something deeper.
The reader registers: this angry kid has a history with this kind of
destruction. The reader doesn't know what it is. They just know the anger has
roots.

**Darek and Kullen.** The twin drift accelerates in barracks. Darek gravitates
toward Kullen because Kullen is warm, sociable, and easy to be around. In the
claustrophobia of barracks life, Darek needs social energy and Kullen provides
it. Through Baret's POV: his brother is spending more time with the lord's son
than with him. Not a betrayal --- just the natural pull of compatible
temperaments. Baret notices and doesn't address it. He's too consumed with his
own internal pressure to engage.

**Sera in barracks.** Sharp, making enemies and allies in equal measure. Her
commentary on the ejection might be the sharpest thing anyone says about it ---
not to Baret specifically, but in his hearing. She names things. That's what she
does. The cohort may be grateful for it or resentful of it depending on the day.

**Garik** channeling his resentment into Proving 1 review and pre-Proving 2
training. Aggressive, competitive. He may clash with Barlas during training ---
two angry young men whose anger has different sources but similar expressions.

**The approaching Proving 2.** The announcements begin. Partners will be
assigned. Routes will be designated. The candidates know they're going into the
field --- real frontier terrain, real danger. The anticipation shifts the
barracks' social dynamics: alliances that mattered in the keep may not matter in
the wilderness. Who you're paired with matters more than who you eat with.

**After Proving 1, candidates are allowed to visit Pakran's town.** This is
established as a privilege --- good behavior, completing Proving 1, earning
limited freedom to leave the keep grounds and enter the town. This town access
is what puts Baret in position to find Elowen in Chapter 6.

**Backstory seeds:** - Barlas hostile and isolated. Payoff: Proving 2 (the
pairing puts two people together who don't want to be together --- the bond
forged in the capsule is more powerful because it had no foundation). - Barlas's
anger with roots. Payoff: Ch 22 (the killing is the roots finally expressed).
The reader's hindsight: "he was always this angry and we could always see why."
- Darek toward Kullen in barracks. Payoff: Book 4 (the friendship Kullen
exploits). - Town access as privilege. Payoff: Ch 6 (Baret in town, finds
Elowen). - The approaching Proving 2. Payoff: Ch 8 (the rigged pairing).

Notes and Flags
---------------

### Barlas Must Be Unlikeable

This is a deliberate inversion of the previous outline. The reader should not
warm to Barlas in Chapter 5. They should be wary of him, maybe actively dislike
him. His anger should feel unstable and threatening. The reversal comes in
Proving 2 when this angry, hostile boy guards Baret through hours of agony in
Ulreth territory. The contrast between who Barlas appears to be and what he's
capable of when it matters is the engine of the character. If the reader already
likes him, the capsule scene confirms what they expected. If the reader dislikes
him, the capsule scene transforms him.

### The Ejection Is Quiet Violence

No beating. No screaming. A family walking through a courtyard with bundles. The
devastation is in the bureaucracy --- technically legal, mathematically precise,
politically targeted. This is harder to write compellingly than physical
violence because there's no obvious villain. But it's the right choice for this
world because it demonstrates what Baret's structural thinking is built to
fight: systems that destroy people through process, not through malice.

### No Home Visits Changes Everything

Candidates are trapped. Maia's voice is a memory, not a presence. Baret can't be
grounded by Tanapun, by the mountain, by the sheep. The isolation amplifies his
internal pressure because there's no release valve. When he finally breaks in
Chapter 6, it's partly because the barracks have given him nowhere to put the
accumulated self-loathing except into action.

### This Is Still the Pressure Chapter

Chapter 5 is all buildup. Nothing breaks. The reader should feel uncomfortable
by the end --- the weight of four inactions, the claustrophobia of barracks,
Barlas's unresolved anger, the quiet aftermath of a family's destruction.
Chapter 6 releases this pressure.

### Pacing

Three scenes across several weeks. Scene 1 (barracks life) establishes the
setting and dynamics. Scene 2 (the ejection) is the chapter's centerpiece ---
the public courtyard scene. Scene 3 (barracks pressure/Barlas) builds the
pressure toward Proving 2 and the town access that sets up Chapter 6. Estimate
4,000-5,500 words.
