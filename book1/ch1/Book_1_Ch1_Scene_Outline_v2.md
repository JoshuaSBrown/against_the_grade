**Against the Grade --- Book 1: Birthright**

**Chapter 1: The Mountain**

Detailed Scene Outline v2

**Chapter Overview**

**POV:** Baret, close third *\[POV/person is an open decision --- see end of
document\]*

**Time:** Early spring morning into late afternoon

**Setting:** Mountain slopes above Tanapun, in the Takdal range. The flock was
grazing at a known high meadow --- south-facing, early grass from snowmelt. One
sheep wandered past the grazing ground into exposed terrain above. The brothers
follow its trail upward.

**Emotional Register:** Quiet and physical → scare/tension spike → warmth and
comedy → grief (unshared) → slow creeping unease on descent

**Purpose:** Introduce Baret, Darek, and the world through mundane action.

**Establishes:** Baret and Darek's relationship (competitive, physical, warm
underneath), their contrasting personalities (gruff/reserved
vs. warm/fastidious), the herding life, the sister's absence (planted without
emphasis), the geode, the lion's claw, the semi-sacred terraforming structures,
the occupation as daily reality, the Ulreth as lived threat, Baret's
terrain-reading as a pre-AI skill, the approaching Marking.

**Locked Decisions:** Sister's death occurred 1 year ago. Tanapun is a fortified
village. Terraforming structures are semi-sacred. The Marking is mentioned in
this chapter. Baret does not respond when Darek mentions the sister.

**Reader Hooks**

**Situation hook:** Two brothers searching for a sheep they can't afford to
lose, in terrain that might kill them. Immediate, concrete stakes.

**Voice hook:** The twin dynamic --- banter, competition, physical comedy,
warmth underneath. Darek's food obsession, Baret's girlish giggle, the bug
scream, the fastidious fussing. If the reader likes these brothers, they'll
follow them anywhere.

**Mystery hook:** The semi-sacred terraforming structures, the strange
landscape, the unresolved follower on the descent. The world has secrets the
characters don't know about.

**End-of-chapter hook:** The being-followed sequence. The reader finishes
Chapter 1 with unease --- something might be out there, the Marking is coming,
and these boys don't fully know what they're walking through.

**Scene 1: The Search (Morning)**

*Value shift: Routine frustration → Tension (pushed toward dangerous terrain)*

| 5 Commandments | Description |
|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Inciting Incident** | The sheep is missing from the high meadow. The brothers are already searching above Tanapun at dawn. The animal matters --- one sheep is the margin Haldrin's taxes leave them. |
| **Progressive Complication** | The search exhausts the easy ground around the meadow. The sheep's trail leads higher, past the semi-sacred terraforming structures, toward the tree line and the Ulreth boundary. The terrain gets stranger and more exposed. The Marking is days away --- Darek jokes about it or Baret registers quiet dread. |
| **Crisis** | Do they keep climbing toward dangerous terrain near the Ulreth boundary, or turn back and accept the loss of a sheep they cannot afford to lose? |
| **Climax** | They keep going. The sheep is worth the risk. A small decision that tells the reader everything about their economic reality. |
| **Resolution** | They're now in exposed, unfamiliar terrain near the boundary. The search continues but the stakes have shifted. |

**Scene Detail**

**Open mid-action.** Baret and Darek are already on the mountain, already tired,
already annoyed. They've been looking since before dawn. The flock was at the
high meadow --- a known south-facing grazing ground where early spring grass
comes in first from snowmelt. One sheep's trail leads up from there into exposed
terrain. Don't explain the economic stakes yet --- let the physical reality do
the work first.

**Baret established through action.** Moves deliberately. Reads the terrain ---
slope angle, wind direction, where an animal would shelter. Closes his eyes for
a beat when he's thinking about where the sheep went, and Darek makes a crack
about him falling asleep. This is herding skill --- the same structural thinking
that will define him later.

**Darek established through contrast.** Talks while he climbs. Fills silence.
Touches a stone outcrop as he passes it the way he touches doorways ---
unconscious habit. He's scanning too, but his attention wanders: a bird
circling, the light on a distant ridge. Competent but not locked in the way
Baret is.

**The fastidious detail.** One quick moment --- Darek adjusting Baret's collar,
or brushing dirt off his brother's pack, or complaining about the state of
something Baret is carrying. Baret ignores it. Where Baret is a slob, Darek
keeps everything tidy and it drives him crazy. One sentence. Establishes the
dynamic.

**The bug.** While scrambling through rocks or undergrowth, something crawls on
Darek. He yelps --- a sound disproportionate to the threat. He swats at himself.
Baret doesn't flinch, maybe rolls his eyes. This is a known thing. On the
frontier, Darek's bug terror is genuinely inconvenient and endlessly amusing.
One to two lines. Comedic beat early in the chapter that establishes the tone as
warm before things get tense.

**The twin dynamic.** Worn-in competition. A moment where one challenges the
other to a short sprint over difficult terrain --- Baret's competitiveness
flashing. The banter is clipped, mountain-dialect rhythm. Short sentences.
Warmth underneath.

**The Marking.** Referenced naturally. Darek jokes about it or Baret's body
language shifts when it comes up. They're heading to Pakran soon. The reader
learns: something is coming that will measure these boys. The Marking gives the
chapter forward pull.

**Tanapun visible below.** A fortified valley settlement, maybe 300 people.
Stone walls, timber palisade --- practical frontier defenses, not grand. The
fortification tells the reader this is a place that expects attack. Smoke from
morning fires. Small enough to see all of in one look.

**Haldrin's occupation as geography.** Patrol routes visible from elevation ---
worn tracks in the hillside. Regular paths between villages. Baret registers
them the way you register power lines --- infrastructure you've never known the
world without.

**The mountain names.** Baret orients using peak names: Takuni (north-facing
peak), Takpin (the ridge). Hard /t/ onsets. Alien-sounding to the reader. The
fingerprint of an engineered language they won't understand until Book 3.

**The forest edge.** Visible in one direction --- dense, darker, extending
toward lower ground. Ulreth territory. Referenced in passing as a boundary they
know not to cross.

**The semi-sacred terraforming structures.** Higher up, the landscape gets
stranger. Rock that isn't quite rock. A ridge that's too regular. A surface
exposed by erosion with faint geometric patterning. The mountain people have
names for these --- they're landmarks and objects of inherited reverence. As the
brothers pass one, a small gesture --- touching it, avoiding stepping on a
particular surface, a brief phrase that reads like habit rather than ceremony.
The way someone crosses themselves passing a church. The reader registers that
these structures carry meaning beyond navigation. One detail suggesting massive
scale --- something disappearing into the mountainside, implying it continues
underground.

**Scene 2: The False Scare (Late Morning)**

*Value shift: Focused search → Fear → Shaky relief*

| 5 Commandments | Description |
|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Inciting Incident** | A sound in the trees or rocks --- wrong pattern. Too heavy, too deliberate. Baret stops. Darek stops because Baret stopped. |
| **Progressive Complication** | The sound continues or shifts. They're exposed, near Ulreth territory. Their sister died in a "Ulreth raid" one year ago. The fear is specific, personal, physical. |
| **Crisis** | Best bad choice: freeze and assess (risking exposure if it IS Ulreth) or run (risking triggering a predator's chase instinct and abandoning the sheep). |
| **Climax** | Baret goes still. Minimal movement, eyes scanning, processing terrain. Darek looks to Baret. Their danger dynamic: Baret reads the situation, Darek reads Baret. |
| **Resolution** | Mundane cause --- rockslide, bird flushing, frost damage. Not a joke resolution. Relief real but incomplete. The danger was plausible. |

**Scene Detail**

Upper slopes, near the tree line. Terrain pushes them closer to the Ulreth
boundary than they'd normally go.

**The fear response as characterization.** Pre-AI pattern recognition in its
rawest form. Baret's stillness, scanning, processing --- the skill set that will
read Ulreth sign in Proving 2, connect his sister's death in Chapter 13, and
identify Haldrin's protocol trap in Chapter 14. Starts here, instinctive.

**What Ulreth mean to them.** Don't explain the Ulreth. Let the fear do the
work. Their sister was reportedly killed in one. The fear is not abstract.

**Darek's response.** He looks to Baret. Their dynamic in danger: Baret reads
the situation, Darek reads Baret. This pattern persists through the entire
series.

**No comedy in this scene.** The bug joke and the banter from Scene 1 are what
makes the fear land --- the contrast between who they were two minutes ago and
who they are now.

**Scene 3: The Sheep, the Morels, the Geode, and the Sister (Midday)**

*Value shift: Relief → Comedy/warmth at full volume → Grief (unshared)*

| 5 Commandments | Description |
|----------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Inciting Incident** | They find the sheep --- sheltered in a rock formation near the high meadow, grazing on early-season growth. The relief is disproportionate. One sheep is margin. |
| **Progressive Complication** | The scare's emotional aftermath opens space. The brothers decompress --- Darek finds morels, comedy and warmth peak (the giggle). Then Darek references their sister. The shift from laughter to grief is sudden --- the air changes. |
| **Crisis** | Irreconcilable goods: Baret can engage with Darek's grief (connection but vulnerability) or stay silent (protection but isolation). This is the emotional crisis of the chapter. |
| **Climax** | Baret does not respond. Not goes quiet --- does not respond. Keeps walking. The silence isn't performed. It's the absence of a response where one should be. Darek doesn't push because this is how Baret has been for a year. |
| **Resolution** | The grief stays unprocessed. The cover story --- Ulreth raid --- is accepted fact. The brothers walk with something between them that neither can name. Baret pockets a geode without much thought. |

**Scene Detail**

The scare has sharpened their senses. They pick up the sheep's trail quickly ---
adrenaline focus. The emotional texture shifts: relief after fear → comedy and
warmth → grief. The comedy makes the grief hit harder.

**The sheep found.** Tracks, droppings, the animal itself. Economic weight
through the brothers' relief. Darek might joke about Haldrin's man and his
counting. Baret's non-response tells the reader the joke is real.

**The morels.** Darek spots a patch of wild morels --- early spring, just after
snowmelt, pushing through the leaf litter. His food obsession kicks in
immediately. He crouches, starts collecting carefully --- the fastidious side on
full display. He lectures Baret on how most people ruin them, how they need low
heat and nothing more than butter, how Maia uses too much salt but he'd never
tell her that (the sole exception to his rule about never criticizing her
cooking --- he won't criticize, but he'll "suggest improvements"). He pockets
them carefully. This is Darek at his most himself --- food is his language of
care.

**The giggle.** Something goes wrong while Darek is collecting --- he slips on
wet ground while fixated on a patch, or a bug crawls out of a mushroom he's
inspecting and he screams. The slapstick catches Baret off guard. The girlish
giggle escapes --- involuntary, embarrassing, a sound that doesn't match the
gruff boy the reader has been watching. Darek immediately pounces. He knows
exactly what that sound means and he wants more of it. He might go for the
ticklish ribs. Baret shoves him off. This is the brothers at full warmth ---
physical, competitive, stupid, loving. The reader needs this moment before the
grief.

**Darek pockets the morels and some extra for later.** He carries food the way
Baret carries the lion's claw. Where Baret carries protection, Darek carries
nourishment. This parallel doesn't need to be stated --- the reader will feel
it.

**The geode.** In the aftermath of the comedy, Baret picks up a rock. Cracks it.
Crystal inside. Pockets it without much thought. Fifteen seconds of page time.
Do not signal importance.

**The sister.** The warmth fades. Darek references her --- the transition is
natural because they've been acting like kids, and the sister was part of that
world. Maybe the morels remind him of something she liked. Maybe the laughter
triggers a memory of her laughing. The shift from comedy to grief should feel
like stepping off a ledge --- sudden, quiet, the air changing.

**Baret does not respond.** This is the locked decision. Not "goes quiet" ---
does not respond. Keeps walking. The silence isn't performed or dramatic. It's
the absence of a response where one should be. Darek doesn't push because this
is how Baret has been for a year. This is a broken emotional processing pattern,
not visible grief. The reader should sense something slightly concerning ---
this wall predates the AI and the series will need to break it.

**The planted clue.** Tied to the sister reference. One specific environmental
detail that doesn't make sense under the cover story but that Baret has no
reason to question yet. Example: in the weeks before his sister died, one of
Haldrin's men was in Tanapun --- horse tied at the far end of the village, near
the family's home, away from the retainer's usual post. On first read:
scene-setting. On reread: the predator establishing a pattern of access. The
detail must be environmental, specific, and casual. Neither Baret nor the reader
flags it.

The mechanism for decoding this clue in Chapter 13: Baret himself --- with
enhanced cognitive processing from the AI activation --- connects patterns in
his own memories. The AI cannot access his memories (hippocampal pathways are
degraded and nonfunctional). Baret's enhanced cognition makes him a better
pattern-reader. He thinks about his sister, and his sharper brain connects
things he already remembers. The AI's role is confirmatory: Baret says "these
timelines don't match" and the AI validates the analysis. The truth comes from
Baret, not from a machine.

**Open Decision: Point of View / Person**

**Status:** Undecided. Current outline assumes close third person.

**Option 1: Third Person Limited (Close), Locked to Baret**

**Advantages:** Dramatic irony --- reader sees past Baret. Planted clues,
terraforming structures, Maia's fear all work best when the reader suspects what
Baret can't see. Baret's blind spots visible without unreliable narrator
problems. Can open to additional POVs in later books.

**Disadvantages:** Slightly less intimate than first person.

**Option 2: First Person (Baret's Voice)**

**Advantages:** Maximum intimacy. Twin banter more immediate. AI's arrival
genuinely disorienting.

**Disadvantages:** Dramatic irony shrinks. Planted clues harder. Five books in
one voice risks claustrophobia. Baret's blind spots harder to write.

**Option 3: Third Person Multiple POV**

**Advantages:** Access to scenes Baret isn't present for. More useful in later
books.

**Disadvantages:** Dilutes Book 1 investment in Baret. Twin separation loses
tension.

**Recommendation:** Third person limited, locked to Baret for Book 1. Open to
selective POVs later.

**Notes and Flags**

**What This Chapter Is NOT**

Not a prologue. Not a hook with a dramatic inciting event. Two brothers looking
for a sheep. The hook is voice, relationship, comedy, the strange world pressing
in at the edges, and unresolved unease. If the twin dynamic doesn't land, the
rest of the book doesn't work.

**Pacing**

Estimate 4,000--6,000 words. If it pushes past 5,500, the morel scene or
backstory seeding is too heavy. The geode and sister reference need to be
lighter than you think. The morel/giggle beat should be 2--3 paragraphs maximum.

**The Planted Clue**

Hardest element. Must be environmental, specific, and casual. Baret's enhanced
cognition (not the AI) connects it in Chapter 13. The AI confirms the analysis
but cannot access his memories.

**The Morels as Character Choice**

Wild morels: plausible in early spring mountains after snowmelt. Prized,
delicate, require careful handling. Darek's fastidiousness and food obsession on
full display. The bug-in-the-mushroom variant gives you a callback to the bug
terror from Scene 1 and a natural giggle trigger.
