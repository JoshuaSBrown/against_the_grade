## ERA 1: EARTH AND TRANSIT

### Earth's Collapse

Earth's civilization faces an AI crisis. The military AI's memory manipulation
capability is weaponized, destroying institutional trust across governments and
organizations. In the terminal phase, a group launches three identical capsules
toward a distant terraformable planet as a survival contingency.

Transit time from Earth to Ekren: unknown (decades to centuries). All three
capsules carry identical equipment: biological production facility, robotic
caretaker systems, educational curriculum, mainframe (teaching AI, symbiont
upload templates, historical archives), activation infrastructure, and life
support.

### Capsule Fates During Transit

Capsule 1: Arrives intact and fully functional.

Capsule 2: Damaged in transit. Core biological systems survive but higher
functions degraded. Will rush through its deployment sequence.

Capsule 3: Infected by the military AI during launch. Safety mechanisms destroy
the life-creation capability but cannot erase the AI --- it has integrated into
core navigation and landing systems. Capsule lands safely with biological
payload destroyed, military AI dormant but data systems intact. Contains
complete historical record of Earth, symbiont specifications, and military AI
design documentation.

## CRITICAL ANALYSIS

### Inconsistencies Requiring Resolution

**1. Timeline arithmetic.** The story is set "approximately 1,300 years after
the founding capsule landing." Terraforming takes \~400 years. Ecosystem seeding
and human emergence adds another \~6 years minimum. That puts the founding
trauma at \~Year 406, and the story at \~Year 1,300 --- meaning human
civilization is \~894 years old, not \~1,300. Multiple documents conflate "years
after capsule landing" with "years of human civilization." These are two
different numbers separated by \~400 years. Pick one as your canonical reference
point and be consistent. Recommendation: use "years after human emergence" as
the story-facing calendar (the Dasin marker), and "years after capsule landing"
only in backstory documents.

**2. Symbiont degradation percentages appear in two versions.** Early documents:
\~30--40% functional / \~15--25% partial / \~40--50% non-functional. Later
recalibration for the 1,100+ year timeline: \~15--25% functional / \~20--30%
partial / \~45--60% non-functional. The later version is correct for your
timeline. Kill the earlier numbers in all documents.

**3. Baret's age at the Marking.** Memory says 16 at the Marking, turning 17 at
the Sorting. Earlier documents say 14. This was updated but older documents
haven't been corrected. The trial cohort description still says "trial-age: 14"
in the Trial Structure reference document.

**4. Barlas/Borek name collision.** Some documents reference "Borek" as the
companion who kills Haldrin. Others use "Barlas." Your current canonical name is
Barlas. Every reference to "Borek" in the project files needs to be updated.

**5. Leorik compromise residue.** The Kullen hidden timeline in some documents
still shows "Leorik compromised during this period." Since the revision that
makes Leorik NOT a memory manipulation victim (Serik fills that role), these
references are wrong. They persist in at least two documents.

**6. Who dies in the Book 2 Ulreth raid?** Documents say "family member killed"
or "someone Baret loved." Nalin is already dead (killed by Kesal, framed as
Ulreth raid, before the story). So who dies in Book 2? This is flagged vaguely
in multiple places but never resolved. You need a name and a relationship. If
it's Maia, the series loses its most distinctive secondary character. If it's a
non-family member, the emotional weight may not justify calling it the
"devastating personal cost." This is a decision you've been deferring, and it's
load-bearing.

**7. Language drift calibration.** Explicitly flagged: sound change rules were
calibrated for 700 years of drift, but the timeline is \~900 years of human
civilization. More erosion and dialect divergence is implied. This isn't just a
document note --- it affects place names, character names, and dialogue
consistency.

### Pacing Problems

**1. Book 3 is still the structural weak point.** You've identified this
yourself. The break-in to Capsule One is supposed to provide the dramatic spine,
but the book's primary function is still setup: faith crisis builds, Tobas
alliance erodes, Murek confrontation fails, Arabela leaves, Leorik exiled,
Kullen reappears. Each individual beat serves the series, but the book's own
dramatic question is unclear. What does Baret want in Book 3 that he either gets
or fails to get by the end? If the answer is "he wants to understand his origins
and use that knowledge to improve the republic," the break-in is the climax ---
but the emotional stakes need to be personal, not institutional.

**2. The 7-year gap (years 10--16) is a black hole.** Baret goes from age 26 to
33 offscreen. Seven years of exile. What does he do? Who is he when he comes
back? A 26-year-old war hero deposed by democracy is a fundamentally different
person than a 34-year-old man who has spent seven years watching his creation
decay from the outside. The reader needs to feel those years, even in summary.
If Baret hasn't grown during the gap, his Book 5 competence feels unearned. If
he has grown, the reader needs to see evidence of it immediately.

**3. Kullen finds Capsule Three too conveniently.** He's exiled in Year 1--2,
discovers the most dangerous artifact on the planet by Year 2--3. That's fast.
The capsule has been sitting there for 1,300 years undiscovered, and a single
angry exile stumbles across it within a year or two of being cast out? You need
either a reason it was findable specifically by someone in Kullen's situation
(directional clue, rumor, ancestral knowledge from Haldrin's line), or you need
to push the discovery later and compress his operational timeline. The current
version reads as plot convenience.

**4. Book 2's war escalation is extremely fast.** Baret overthrows a regional
lord at age 18. Within a year, he's fighting the monarch. The feudal system has
presumably existed for centuries --- it collapses in under two years? The speed
needs justification. The AI military advantage helps, but the political
coalescence of anti-feudal forces across an entire territory needs more
groundwork. Leorik arriving with his own forces helps, but why does Leorik join
an 18-year-old nobody? What has Baret done between overthrowing Haldrin and the
war starting that makes regional allies take him seriously?

**5. The Serik/senate stabbing comes very late in Book 5.** If the "mystery
breaks open" only when Serik stabs someone on the senate floor, and this happens
in Year 16--17, the early chapters of Book 5 risk being pure
exile-and-observation. The reader needs tension before the mystery breaks ---
Elowen's reports should be creating mounting unease, not just delivering data
points. Consider whether Baret suspects something is wrong earlier and the
stabbing confirms it, rather than the stabbing being the first anomaly he
notices.

### Structural Tension Recommendations

**1. Give Kullen a breadcrumb trail, not a lucky find.** Haldrin's family could
have oral traditions about "the third place" or "the place that bites" ---
fragmentary knowledge passed down from the feudal aristocracy's own incomplete
understanding of capsule lore. Kullen doesn't stumble onto Capsule Three; he
follows a thread that only someone from Haldrin's line would have. This
strengthens the thematic irony: feudal privilege gives Kullen the map to the
weapon that will destroy the republic that replaced feudalism.

**2. Make the gap years visible through Elowen's POV.** If you're expanding to
additional POVs in Books 3--4, Elowen's perspective during the gap years is a
natural Book 5 structural tool. Short, tense chapters from inside the decaying
republic --- watching people she knew change, unable to explain why, unable to
leave, sending reports to a man in exile who can't act on them. This transforms
the gap from dead time into a slow-burn spy thriller.

**3. Book 3 needs a ticking clock.** The faith crisis, the oligarch
confrontation, and the Capsule One break-in are all present, but nothing forces
urgency. What happens if Baret doesn't act by a certain point? If Tobas is about
to seal off Capsule One permanently (consecrate it, bury it, build over the
accessible chambers), Baret's break-in becomes a heist under deadline rather
than a philosophical decision.

**4. Seed the neighboring kingdom earlier.** Leorik flees to an unnamed
neighboring kingdom after Book 3 exile and rises to senior military command. By
Book 5 he's attacking the capital with a foreign army. The kingdom needs to
exist in the reader's awareness from Book 2 at the latest --- as a political
neighbor, a trade partner, a source of tension during the war. If the kingdom
only appears when it's needed for Leorik's arc, it feels like a plot device.

**5. The Darek realization needs physical presence, not just intellectual
understanding.** Baret learning that Darek was a victim of memory manipulation
is described as the "lowest point in the series." But if Darek is offscreen for
seven years and Baret only learns the truth through pattern analysis, the
emotional impact is cerebral, not visceral. Consider whether Baret sees Darek
before learning the truth --- maybe a brief, bitter encounter during the exile
that the reader rereads differently later. The regret needs a face.

### Open Questions the Timeline Exposes

1.  What is the Dasin marker? Year 0 of the human calendar. Is it the capsule
    landing, the human emergence, or the attack? Each choice has different
    implications for how the culture understands itself.

2.  What does Baret do during the 7-year exile? This is the single biggest
    structural gap in the series.

3.  Where is the neighboring kingdom geographically? Its military capacity, its
    relationship to the Ulreth frontier, and its political system all need to
    exist before Leorik arrives there.

4.  Nalin's death timeline relative to the story. She's killed by Kesal before
    Book 1. How long before? If it's recent (within a year or two), Maia's
    behavior is fresh grief under control. If it's years ago, it's scar tissue
    --- different emotional texture.

5.  Does the Capsule 2 gatekeeping institution have a name? It's referenced
    structurally but never named. An institution this important to the series'
    political tension needs identity.
