The Ulreth --- Species Reference Document
=========================================

1. Origin
---------

The Ulreth are not a separate species. They are an unfinished draft ---
intermediate-stage organisms from Capsule Two's rushed biological production
sequence. The same sequence that was designed to progress from simple ecosystem
organisms through complex fauna to humans instead terminated early, producing
organisms that matured quickly and were physically robust but cognitively
underdeveloped compared to the human endpoint.

Capsule Two was damaged in transit. The nature of the damage caused errors in
its biological production sequence. Rather than completing the full staged
deployment, the capsule rushed through intermediate stages and released
organisms that should never have existed independently. The Ulreth are the most
dramatic and recognizable failure, but Capsule Two's rushed sequence also
released slightly wrong organisms at every stage --- predators that were too
aggressive, herbivores with incorrect metabolic rates, organisms that almost
worked but didn't regulate correctly in the wild. These errors have been
propagating through the planet's food webs for over a millennium.

The Ulreth were produced approximately a century before Capsule One produced
humans. By the time humans emerged, the Ulreth were already established and
territorial. The Ulreth attacked Capsule One's facility not out of strategy but
out of resource-seeking instinct --- scavenging, not invading. They didn't
understand what the facility was. The attack destroyed Capsule One's robotic
caretakers, educational infrastructure, and activation systems, stranding an
entire human civilization without knowledge of its origins.

The Ulreth share a biological origin with humans. Both were produced by capsule
technology launched from Earth. This fact is the truth underlying the Kinship
heresy --- the most dangerous and most suppressed heterodox tradition within the
Interrupted Promise faith.

2. Biology and Intelligence
---------------------------

### Physical Characteristics

The Ulreth are physically robust, territorial, and instinct-driven. They exist
on a biological spectrum from barely animal to disturbingly close to human.
Short lifespans drive aggressive proliferation --- they are r-strategists,
producing many offspring with high mortality.

They lack the cognitive architecture for the symbiont interface. Capsule Two's
activation infrastructure exists and is functional, but the Ulreth are the wrong
species for the hardware --- the sequence that produced them terminated before
reaching the stage where symbiont compatibility would have been encoded.

### Intelligence Spectrum

**Decided: Near-sapient (early hominin level) at the upper end.**

Most Ulreth operate at a high-animal level --- pack coordination, territorial
behavior, learned hunting patterns. The outliers, roughly 10--15% of the
population, display behaviors that unsettle anyone who witnesses them:

**Tool crafting, not just tool use.** Shaping materials into implements with
observable forethought. Not sophisticated --- no hafting, no composite tools ---
but demonstrating a cognitive step beyond any animal on Earth.

**Cooperative behavior beyond pack instinct.** What appears to be division of
labor during raids. Individuals taking roles that suggest planning rather than
instinct. Flanking maneuvers that require anticipating human responses.

**Death-related behavior.** Something that resembles mourning --- lingering near
dead Ulreth, vocalizations that change character, behavior that doesn't serve
survival. Not ritual. Not burial. But something that doesn't fit "mindless
animal."

**Proto-communication.** Repeated vocalizations with apparent contextual
meaning. Not language. Possibly a precursor. Frontier people who've spent years
listening know the sounds mean something. No human has decoded any of it, and
most who hear it described dismiss it as animal noise.

**Critical absences.** No fire. No architecture beyond temporary shelters. No
art. No visible ritual beyond death-related behavior. No agriculture or food
storage. These absences are what let mainstream human society maintain the
"they're just animals" position. The near-sapient behaviors are episodic and
ambiguous enough that anyone who wants to can explain them away.

3. Population and Territory
---------------------------

### Planetary Geography

The Ulreth occupy a connected landmass centered on Capsule Two's location (pixel
coordinates 1186, 1253 on the global map). The corrected land mask shows this
landmass at approximately 10.9 million square miles, connected to the southern
continent via a narrow land bridge of thin peninsulas and island chains.

Planet diameter: 7,700 miles. Map scale: 12.1 miles per pixel. Global land
coverage: \~25% land / \~75% ocean.

### Expansion Model

Terrain-aware Dijkstra diffusion from the capsule point:

-   Base expansion rate: \~10 miles per year (conservative for degraded terrain)
-   Terrain difficulty: lowland easy, mountain hard, no water crossings
    (pre-agricultural species)
-   Full landmass coverage reached by approximately Year 400
-   At carrying capacity across the entire connected landmass by approximately
    Year 600

### Population at Story Time (Year \~1,000)

At the default carrying density of 0.25 per square mile:

-   **Population: \~2.7 million Ulreth**
-   **Territory: \~10.9 million square miles** (entire connected landmass
    saturated)
-   **\~90,000 bands** (at \~30 per band)
-   **Human-to-Ulreth ratio: \~2.9:1** (against \~8 million humans)

### Population as a Narrative Lever

The carrying density is the primary adjustable parameter. The degraded Capsule
Two ecosystem --- every stage of its rushed biological sequence released
slightly wrong organisms --- provides justification for lower density:

  Carrying Density   Total Population   Ratio (vs 8M humans)   Narrative Scale
  ------------------ ------------------ ---------------------- -----------------------------------
  0.25 / sq mi       \~2,700,000        2.9:1                  Civilizational existential threat
  0.10 / sq mi       \~1,090,000        7.3:1                  Major regional power
  0.05 / sq mi       \~545,000          14.7:1                 Serious frontier problem
  0.025 / sq mi      \~273,000          29.3:1                 Persistent military challenge

**Decision needed:** Which population scale best serves the story? The larger
the Ulreth population, the more justified Haldrin's defensive posture and the
more devastating Kullen's frontier sabotage in Book 5.

4. Relationship to Human Society
--------------------------------

### Frontier Culture

People who live on the Ulreth border are different from the rest of the kingdom.
Harder, more pragmatic, holding opinions about the Ulreth that polite society
finds distasteful or delusional depending on which direction those opinions go.

**Dominant frontier view: dangerous pests, kill on sight.** Most border
communities have lost people to Ulreth raids. They don't philosophize about
Ulreth intelligence because abstract questions feel obscene when your neighbor
was dragged out of a farmstead at night. This view aligns with the feudal lord's
military posture.

**Minority frontier view: something's wrong with the story we tell ourselves.**
Old scouts, veteran soldiers, hunters who've spent decades in the borderlands.
They don't necessarily have a coherent theory --- they just know that what
they've seen doesn't match "dangerous animal." These people are the living
source material for the Kinship heresy, whether or not they've ever heard of it.

**Urban society's view: irrelevant frontier problem.** People in the capital and
secondary cities have never seen a Ulreth. The border conflict is a distant tax
burden and a source of adventure stories. The Ulreth are bogeymen --- useful for
scaring children and justifying the frontier lord's military budget. This
disconnect means the protagonist's civil war coalition could genuinely
underestimate the Ulreth threat.

### The Feudal Lord's Legitimacy

Haldrin's legitimacy rests heavily on Ulreth defense, and he is not wrong about
the threat. His soldiers have firsthand experience with an enemy that adapts,
coordinates, and is functionally impossible to eliminate. His brutality toward
his own people exists in the context of governing a frontier territory under
permanent low-intensity warfare against a near-sapient enemy. He is a hard man
because the situation genuinely demands hard choices.

This makes Baret's arc more morally complex. Overthrowing the lord isn't
overthrowing a simple tyrant --- it's dismantling the one power structure that
was actually managing the Ulreth problem, imperfectly and cruelly, but managing
it. The village massacre when guards are pulled isn't just tragic. It's a direct
consequence of Baret not understanding (or not caring about) a problem he
dismissed as exaggerated feudal propaganda.

5. The Kinship Heresy
---------------------

The most dangerous heterodox tradition within the Interrupted Promise and the
most actively suppressed.

**Core position:** The Ulreth are also children of the metal mothers ---
incomplete in a different way, interrupted at an earlier stage. They were made
first. They suffered first. They are the most incomplete of all.

**Geographic concentration:** Underground. A handful of scholars, frontier
people who have observed Ulreth behavior too closely and noticed things that
don't fit the "mindless interrupter" narrative, and a few theologians who
followed the internal logic of the interrupted-promise doctrine to its
uncomfortable conclusion: if the metal mothers created all life, and if the
Ulreth are alive, then the Ulreth are part of the design.

**Why it's suppressed:** Orthodox doctrine requires the Ulreth to be
fundamentally other --- not part of the design, not children of the metal
mothers, not deserving of moral consideration. The Ulreth-as-interrupters is not
just a theological point. It is the justification for territorial expansion, for
violence against Ulreth populations, for treating an entire species as a threat
to be managed rather than a people to be understood. A tradition that says "they
are our kin" is not just heretical. It is politically treasonous because it
undermines the moral framework that permits organized violence against them.

**Narrative function:** The Kinship heresy is closer to the truth than any other
theological position. When Baret discovers the shared capsule origin, this
suppressed tradition is retroactively vindicated. The people who were persecuted
for claiming the Ulreth were kin turn out to have been right. The most dangerous
heresy was the closest to truth, and orthodoxy suppressed it not because the
evidence was weak but because the implications were destabilizing.

6. Narrative Role Across the Series
-----------------------------------

### Book 1 --- Immediate Physical Threat

The Ulreth are the background danger that defines frontier life and justifies
Haldrin's brutal system. Baret encounters them near Capsule Two during the
Provings. The Ulreth raiding party that fractures Proving 3 Round 3 is the first
direct, visceral encounter with them. Kullen fights alongside Baret during this
raid.

The Ulreth function here as environmental pressure --- they don't have
motivations the reader needs to understand, just physical threat that shapes
human political structures. Haldrin's trial system exists because the Ulreth
exist.

### Book 2 --- Consequence of Protagonist's Choices

**The village raid (Chapter 15).** Baret pulls guards from his home village to
the front line during the war against the monarchy. Rational military
calculation --- the village is behind friendly lines, the monarch is the bigger
immediate threat. The Ulreth don't respect front lines. They raid the undefended
village. Baret's father dies.

This is the ideological wound of the series: Haldrin was brutal and wrong about
many things, but he wasn't wrong about the Ulreth being a real threat requiring
organized defense. Baret's triumph created the conditions for the attack.

**The ambiguous encounter (Chapter 17).** Baret witnesses something that doesn't
fit the "mindless monster" narrative. \[Gap: specific encounter not yet decided.
Options: mourning behavior over Ulreth dead from the village raid; a
near-sapient individual making a deliberate non-hostile choice; tool crafting
beyond animal capability; proto-communication.\]

**Capsule Two access.** The Ulreth's territorial presence around Capsule Two
becomes a geopolitical problem. Who gets to make the dangerous journey through
Ulreth territory for activation? Access to activation becomes a power question
--- the inequality engine restarting in a new form.

### Book 3 --- Political Detonation via Capsule One Data

**New insertion point.** When Baret accesses Capsule One's mainframe, the
partial data recovery includes records about Capsule Two's biological output ---
what it was supposed to produce versus what it actually produced. Baret doesn't
get the full picture (the data is fragmentary), but he gets enough to realize
the Ulreth aren't random wildlife. They're a failed output of the same system
that made humans. The Kinship heresy confirmed by hard evidence.

This detonates in three directions simultaneously:

**Tobas** needs the Ulreth to be "Interrupters" --- fundamentally other ---
because that's orthodox doctrine. If they're kin, his entire theological
framework around the mark and divine design cracks.

**Murek** sees Ulreth territory as potential economic expansion space. If the
Ulreth have legitimate claim to the land, that's an obstacle.

**Frontier communities** who've been killing Ulreth for centuries face a
retroactive moral crisis.

Baret possessing this information and deciding what to do with it --- suppress
it, release it, use it as leverage --- is a concrete decision with real stakes
that ties directly into his core principle of truth over safety. This helps
solve Book 3's identified lull problem by adding a politically dangerous
discovery with immediate, tangible consequences alongside the seed vault
discovery.

### Book 4 --- Populist Wedge Issue and Exile Encounter

**The Ulreth question as political weapon.** The Capsule One data leaks (because
Baret is constitutionally incapable of suppressing truth). The Ulreth question
becomes politically weaponizable by Kullen's populist movement from both
directions:

Frontier communities are furious that elites are now calling the Ulreth "kin"
when it's frontier families who've been dying to Ulreth raids for generations.
Urban progressives use the Kinship revelation to paint the frontier military as
barbaric. This splits the coalition --- the same dynamic as real-world
environmental and indigenous rights issues fracturing working-class and
progressive alliances. The Ulreth don't need to appear on-page for this to work,
but the debate about them should be a source of political friction contributing
to the populist wave that ousts Baret.

**Exile through Ulreth territory.** When Baret is couped and Elowen hides him,
his escape route could require crossing the frontier into or near Ulreth-held
land. Baret, now carrying the knowledge that they share an origin, navigates
their territory with that understanding coloring everything he sees. A short
sequence --- not a subplot --- that grounds the political abstraction in
physical reality and gives the reader a visceral reminder of what the Ulreth
actually are.

### Book 5 --- Civilizational Consequence of Institutional Collapse

**Kullen's frontier sabotage.** Kullen has Capsule Three's complete records and
knows exactly what the Ulreth are and what they were supposed to be. His
strategy: use memory manipulation to compromise officials managing frontier
defense, causing it to degrade incrementally over the gap years. A garrison
commander redeploys troops to a different sector. A supply official
deprioritizes border fortifications. A regional governor cuts the frontier
military budget for urban infrastructure. Each decision looks individually
rational --- the kinds of tradeoffs governments make all the time. Nobody
connects the dots because the Ulreth pressure builds slowly.

Raids increase from occasional to frequent to devastating. By the time anyone
recognizes the pattern, Kullen's populist movement is already positioned as the
only faction taking the threat seriously.

**The structural echo.** Haldrin maintained the Ulreth threat as justification
for his brutal system --- "you need me because the Ulreth are dangerous." Kullen
amplifies the threat to delegitimize the existing order --- "you need me because
the current leaders can't protect you from the Ulreth." Father and son, same
instrument, opposite direction. The underlying logic is identical: whoever
controls the narrative around the Ulreth controls the population.

**The moral horror.** Kullen let real people die to real Ulreth attacks to fuel
his political ascent. Frontier villages destroyed, families killed --- not as
collateral damage but as strategy. And Kullen can justify it with four books of
evidence: every system failed, every leader was inadequate, the only way to save
humanity is to take control, and a few hundred frontier deaths now prevent
civilizational collapse later. He's doing the math that Baret has always refused
to do --- deciding who is expendable.

**The existential stakes.** Even if Baret defeats Kullen, he still has to deal
with the Ulreth frontier that Kullen's sabotage has destabilized. 2.7 million
r-strategist near-sapient creatures will simply expand into any vacuum. The same
thing that happened in Book 2 (guards pulled, village raided) happens at
civilizational scale. The Ulreth are the constant --- every time humans weaken
their own institutions through internal conflict, the Ulreth pressure fills the
gap, indifferent to human politics.

8. Thematic Function
--------------------

The Ulreth are the answer to "does freedom require equality?" that nobody wants
to hear. If Ulreth share human origin but aren't human, where do they fall in
the moral framework? Every faction gets this wrong in a different way that
reflects their core failure:

-   **Haldrin** treated them as a military problem --- triage logic, acceptable
    losses
-   **Tobas** treated them as theological props --- "Interrupters" who validate
    orthodox doctrine
-   **Murek** treated them as a resource question --- Ulreth territory as
    expansion space
-   **The populist movement** treats them as a political football --- frontier
    deaths as ammunition
-   **Kullen** treats them as a strategic tool --- engineering the crisis,
    deciding who is expendable

Baret is the only person who might get it right, but "right" is genuinely
unclear --- you can't negotiate with near-sapient creatures, you can't
exterminate kin, and you can't ignore 2.7 million of them. That unresolvable
tension is exactly the kind of costly compromise the series ending is built for.

The Ulreth never change across five books. They are 2.7 million near-sapient
creatures doing what they've always done --- expanding into available territory,
competing for resources. Every human faction projects its own politics onto
them. The species that is genuinely beyond human categories of freedom and
equality becomes the mirror in which every human system reveals its failure
mode.

9. Open Gaps
------------

-   **Carrying density decision.** The 0.25/sq mi default produces 2.7 million
    --- civilizational scale. Lower densities are defensible via the degraded
    Capsule Two ecosystem. The choice affects how justified Haldrin's posture is
    and how devastating Kullen's sabotage becomes.
-   **Book 2 ambiguous encounter specifics.** Must be concrete enough to haunt,
    ambiguous enough not to resolve. Best candidate: mourning behavior over
    Ulreth dead from the village raid, because it connects the personal loss to
    the unsettling observation.
-   **Book 3 Capsule One data --- how much about the Ulreth?** Fragmentary
    enough to confirm shared origin without giving Baret the full picture. The
    political explosion comes from the confirmation, not from comprehensive
    understanding.
-   **Book 4 exile route.** Does it cross Ulreth territory? If so, how long, and
    what does Baret witness?
-   **Book 5 frontier collapse specifics.** Which communities are destroyed? Are
    any named characters among the casualties? The emotional weight of Kullen's
    sabotage depends on the reader knowing the victims.
-   **Neighboring kingdom's relationship to Ulreth.** Leorik flees to a
    neighboring kingdom after exile. Does that kingdom share a Ulreth border? If
    so, their military posture and political relationship to the Ulreth question
    would color Leorik's exile and return.
-   **Human settlement landmass.** Confirmed that Capsule One produced humans on
    a different part of the planetary geography. The specific human landmass and
    its relationship to the Ulreth landmass (same connected body? across a
    strait? different continent entirely?) affects the border conflict's
    physical geography.
