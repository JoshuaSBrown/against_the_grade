MAIA --- Baret's Mother
-----------------------

**Status:** ⚠️ Personality now well-sketched / ⚠️ Plot role partially defined

**Name:** Maia (from seed language /m/ onset --- quantities/relations category.
Ma-ia, with vowel hiatus from lost /h/ between vowels. Marks the name as very
old, almost fossilized seed-language. A mother's name that sounds like it comes
from the beginning of the world)

**Background:** Alive and present throughout the series. Compassionate, strong,
and a little bit crazy. Knows the truth about her daughter's death and has
carried that while raising her sons. The grief hasn't broken her --- it's made
her fiercer.

**Personality:** Ferocious warmth. She's a strong mother who expresses love
through controlled chaos. Throws food and sandals at her boys when they pester
her too much --- which they sometimes do deliberately because it's funny. She
once threw an apple at one of the lord's guards after he splashed mud on her
while she was walking down the road, then hid before he could turn around. She's
not afraid of power --- she's afraid of getting caught, which is a different
thing entirely. Her sons got their nerve from somewhere.

**Quirk:** She loves really bright colors. Everywhere. No subtlety whatsoever.
She tries to make clothes for her kids but they are always garish --- clashing,
loud, aggressively cheerful. Her boys just groan. This only becomes truly
apparent in the later books when Baret actually has some wealth, because when
the family had nothing she couldn't inflict her taste on anyone. The moment
resources are available, she's unleashed. Her sons have survived war, feudal
overthrow, and political crisis, and the thing that defeats them is their
mother's color sense.

**Strength:** She holds the family together. Not through strategy or principle
--- through sheer force of personality and refusal to let grief consume what she
still has. She carried the truth about her daughter's death while raising two
sons in a system that killed her daughter, and she didn't break. She threw
sandals instead.

**Weakness:** The grief she carries about her daughter. She's never had justice,
never had acknowledgment, and the secret has shaped the family in ways that
aren't fully visible. Whether Baret's discovery of the truth in Book 1 changes
his relationship with her --- whether she's relieved or terrified that he knows
--- is an open question.
