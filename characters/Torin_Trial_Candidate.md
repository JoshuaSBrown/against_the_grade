TORIN BAKRIN NOR'SULAK --- Trial Candidate
==========================================

**Status:** ⚠️ Role defined / ❌ Personality minimal

**Name:** Torin Bakrin Nor'Sulak. /t/ onset (terrain/geography). To-rin, CV-CVC.
Surname Bakrin (stone worker). Patronymic from Sulak (old mountain). Full
three-part commoner name.

**Age:** 17 at start of Book 1

**Background:** A stonemason's son from an old mountain family. Commoner, same
region as Baret. Enters the trials alongside Baret, Darek, Kullen, and the other
candidates.

Role in the Trials
------------------

Physically strong, good endurance, not fast. The kind of candidate who performs
steadily but never brilliantly. Useful in the Provings as a reliable body ---
someone Baret absorbs in Round 2 or 3 who follows orders without complaint. Not
a leader. The backbone of any team he's on. When Baret's group grows across
Proving 3, Torin is the workhorse --- dependable, unglamorous, essential.

Strength
--------

Reliability. He shows up, he does the work, he doesn't break. In a trial system
designed to identify brilliance and sort out failures, Torin is neither --- he's
the steady middle that every functional group actually runs on.

Weakness
--------

He doesn't stand out. In a system that rewards exceptional performance, being
reliable is invisible. Assessors don't notice him. Leaders don't recruit him
first. He gets absorbed, not chosen.
