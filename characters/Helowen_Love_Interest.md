ELOWEN --- The Cousin / Primary Love Interest
---------------------------------------------

**Status:** ⚠️ Thematic role clear / Personal detail minimal

**Name:** Elowen (formally Helowen, from seed language /h/ onset ---
identity/abstract category, with feminine /h/-drop convention. He-lo-wen,
CV-CV-CVC. The /w/ is a glide from original /u/ adjacent to another vowel)

**Background:** From a diminished branch of the betrothed's family --- old name,
no money, pride without resources. Understands what it means to have worth the
system doesn't recognize.

**Core Arc:**

-   **Book 1:** Present in the background. Baret defends her from village youths
    who are picking on her --- his first act of leadership, and an act of
    kindness that wins her heart. He doesn't know the significance of what he's
    done. He gives her something (the token) in this interaction or shortly
    after.
-   **Books 2--3:** Quietly supportive while Baret chases the betrothed. Not
    passive --- patient. Knows her own worth and is willing to wait for Baret to
    figure it out.
-   **Book 4:** Baret is in exile, at his lowest. Elowen hides him (safe house).
    She returns the token --- the moment when she shows him who he's always
    been. This is the romantic turning point.
-   **Book 5:** Baret's partner. Her role in the investigation and climax is
    undefined.

**Personality:** Stoic. Reserved. Believes in propriety --- in doing things
correctly, in maintaining composure, in never letting anyone see you crack. Even
when her feelings are overwhelming, she holds them behind a wall of decorum.
This isn't coldness --- it's discipline forged from experience.

**What She Carries:** An object given to her by a friend she betrayed when she
was younger. The specifics of the betrayal and the object are undefined, but the
function is clear: it's a reminder of the worst thing she's done, and everything
she does since is an attempt at redemption. Her stoicism isn't just personality
--- it's penance. She controls herself because she knows what happens when she
doesn't.

**Strength:** Composure under pressure. When everyone else is panicking, Elowen
is still functioning. She's the person who keeps logistics running, who doesn't
flinch, who makes the hard call without needing to process it first. In a crisis
she's the most valuable person in the room.

**Weakness:** She hides what matters to avoid losing face. Her propriety is also
a cage. She won't advocate for herself, won't express what she wants, won't risk
the vulnerability of being seen clearly. The betrayal in her past taught her
that acting on strong feelings leads to harm, so she suppresses all of them ---
including the ones that would serve her. When she finally breaks through this
(the token return in Book 4), it's devastating precisely because it costs her
everything she's built her identity around.

**Quirk:** She loves perfumes and has a hobby of trying to make them --- but
they always smell absolutely horrible. Every attempt is a disaster. The contrast
with her stoicism and obsession with propriety is the joke: the most composed,
proper person in the cast walks around smelling like a failed experiment. It
also gives her something entirely her own, separate from Baret, separate from
the cousin dynamic --- a private passion she's terrible at and keeps doing
anyway, which says something about her stubbornness underneath the composure.

-   Her own agency in the plot --- she needs independent goals and conflicts

-   Her role in Books 2, 3, and 5 beyond "supportive presence"
-   Her relationship to Arabela (they're cousins --- how does the betrayal
