## BARLAS --- Baret's Companion

**Status:** ⚠️ Defining act established / ✅ Personality and backstory developed

**Name:** Barlas (from seed language /b/ onset --- substance/resources
category. Bar-las, CVC-CVC. "Bonded substance" --- loyal material. A
blunt, solid name for someone defined by one irreversible act)

**Background:** One of Baret's close allies. His family was left on
foot and abandoned when a Gulrek force was found approaching. Haldrin
was on the patrol with them and personally made the decision to fall
back to the walls --- a better defensive position. Barlas's family
couldn't keep pace. Barlas survived by hiding but witnessed his family
massacred and eaten. Haldrin made the tactically correct call: defending
the walls saves more people than dying in the open with civilians who
can't keep up. He probably doesn't remember the specific family. It
was one triage decision among hundreds. Barlas remembers every second of
it.

**What He Carries:** A scar from being whipped as a child for getting in
the way --- an earlier, smaller cruelty from the same system. But the
real weight he carries is invisible: the memory of hiding while his
family was torn apart, and the knowledge that the man responsible
considered it an acceptable cost. The scar is what people see. The
massacre is what drives him.

**Quirk:** Smacks his friends on the backside as a sign of camaraderie.
It's physical, affectionate, slightly annoying --- the kind of thing
that makes him feel real on the page. It also signals something about
Barlas: his loyalty is expressed through contact, through presence,
through physicality rather than words. He's also a womanizer --- always
chasing someone, always with a story, never serious about it. The
contrast between his absolute loyalty to Baret and his complete
inability to commit to a woman tells you everything about where his
heart actually lives. It also makes him more likeable on the page, which
makes the murder more shocking when it comes. He absolutely hates
vegetables and won't eat anything green. He believes mutton is the only
true meat --- everything else is a compromise. He'll argue this point
with anyone, at length, with total conviction.

**Strength:** Loyalty. Absolute, unquestioning, bone-deep. When Barlas
decides you're his, he'll walk into fire for you without hesitating.
In battle he's the most reliable person at Baret's side. Physically fierce --- high endurance, high pain tolerance, instinct-driven. Not a strategist but an executor who makes decisions decisive through sheer ferocity and refusal to lose.

**Weakness:** An undercurrent of explosive anger triggered by injustice.
He's unpredictable --- fine for hours, days, weeks, and then something
tips him and the violence comes out fast and total. The murder of
Haldrin after surrender isn't an aberration. It's Barlas standing in
front of the man who left his family to die --- and the man doesn't
even know who he is. Haldrin probably has no idea why he's being
killed. To Haldrin it was a tactical decision he barely remembers. To
Barlas it's the defining moment of his life. His loyalty and his rage
are the same energy pointed in different directions, and you never know
which way it'll go until it's too late.

**Book 1 Trial Role:**

- **Marking:** Present among the trial candidates at Pakran. First impression for Baret --- someone he doesn't yet know well. Fierce, aggressive, instinct-driven. Not someone who calculates.
- **Proving 1 (Individual):** Emerges as a physical presence. High endurance, high pain tolerance. Raw intensity that assessors notice. Personality forming through cohort interactions --- the womanizing, the mutton arguments, the backside-smacking camaraderie.
- **Between Provings:** Becomes a real presence in Baret's life. His grievance against Haldrin exists as subtext, personal history not yet foregrounded. When Baret defends Elowen (Chapter 6), Barlas sees a kindred spirit --- someone who acts on what they feel.
- **Proving 2 (Partners):** Paired with Baret by assessors. On paper: Baret's terrain skills complement Barlas's physical aggression. In practice: two expendables packaged together, routed into Gulrek-adjacent terrain as a death sentence disguised as a challenging assignment. **The terrain is the same kind of landscape where Barlas's family was massacred.** Barlas doesn't say it, but the ground means something specific.
  - Baret saves Barlas from a Gulrek attack (Chapter 9) --- herding instincts, no enhancement. Barlas owes Baret his life.
  - At Capsule Two (Chapter 10): Baret is activated --- hours of agony, convulsing, screaming, helpless. In Gulrek territory, the same kind of territory where Barlas's family was killed. Barlas could run. Barlas stays. Guards Baret through hours of agony. **The defining act of loyalty.**
  - Barlas touches the same infrastructure. Silence. The technology assessed Barlas's symbiont architecture and found nothing functional enough.
  - Returns to Proving 2 covering for Baret's disorientation. Deflecting attention, explaining away erratic behavior. Loyal. Always loyal.
- **Between Provings 2 and 3:** Trains alongside Baret. Watches Baret develop AI-enhanced capabilities. Compensates with effort for what Baret received by lottery. Fierce, determined. Not lesser --- someone who has to work for everything Baret was given. Hears about the rigged trials through Baret. His anger is simpler, more direct: he doesn't need an AI to know he wants Haldrin destroyed.
- **Proving 3 Round 2:** On a different team from Baret. The team leader makes a bad call --- freezes under pressure, miscalculates a route. Leadership failure puts the team in a vulnerable position. A real Gulrek encounter turns vulnerability into crisis. **Barlas holds the remnants together through ferocity and determination.** Brings them to Baret's group. Integrates smoothly --- the Proving 2 bond is immediate, trust without hesitation. The rest of Barlas's team sees Barlas snap into Baret's command and follows.
- **The Sorting:** Responds to Baret's palm-inward gesture instantly. Of course.
- **The Killing (Chapter 22):** After Haldrin surrenders, Barlas kills him. Stands in front of the man who left his family to die. **Haldrin doesn't know who Barlas is.** One triage decision among hundreds. To Haldrin, a tactical footnote. To Barlas, the defining moment of his life. The killing is loyalty (Haldrin tried to murder Baret), rage (Haldrin left his family to die), and the capsule's silence (he touched the same technology and got nothing) --- all expressed as violence by someone without Baret's processing buffer. Not planned. Barlas acts on what he feels.

**Core Arc:**

- **Book 1:** Forged through Proving 2 and the capsule. Fights the rebellion with no enhancement --- every swing fueled by the capsule's silence and a family left to die. After the lord surrenders, kills him. The defining act of the character. It stains Baret's greatest triumph and shapes his approach to governance forever.
- **Books 2+:** Undefined. Does this character remain relevant, or
    does he serve his narrative purpose in Book 1?
