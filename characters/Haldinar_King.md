KING HALDANUR NOR'GALRIK --- The Monarch
----------------------------------------

**Status:** ⚠️ Personality well-sketched / ❌ Arc and plot role undefined

**Name:** Haldanur Nor'Galrik (from seed language /h/ onset ---
governance/abstract category. Hal-da-nur, CVC-CV-CVC. Father's name Galrik has
/g/ onset --- conflict/boundaries, which says something about the dynasty's
history)

**Background:** The king. Rules above the feudal lords like Haldrin. A man of
the court rather than the frontier.

**Personality:** Political and clever in the way of education --- not a
battlefield leader but a master of the court. Extremely generous to those loyal
to him, which buys devotion that's hard to break. His political enemies are
known to wake up dead --- never traced to him, but everyone knows. This is a man
who smiles at you over wine while someone else handles the unpleasantness, and
who would be genuinely offended if you accused him of it.

**What He Carries:** A fondness for wine. He's quick to see the good in people
who gift it to him --- a detail that tells you exactly how his court operates.
Loyalty is transactional, and the currency is knowing what the king likes. He
loves horse racing --- spectacle, competition, the display of fine animals and
finer wagers.

**Quirk:** No tolerance for tardiness. A horrible temper that he mostly controls
--- but his eye twitches when he's about to lose his cool. Everyone in his court
knows the twitch. It's the signal to stop talking, step back, or leave the room.
The twitch is the kind of detail that makes scenes electric --- a character
notices it and the reader knows exactly what's about to happen.

**Strength:** He understands people as political instruments. He knows who can
be bought, who can be frightened, who needs flattery, who needs threat. His
generosity isn't kindness --- it's investment. His court runs smoothly because
everyone in it has been correctly assessed and correctly compensated. He's a
transactional genius.

**Weakness:** His temper. The political sophistication, the educated cleverness,
the generosity --- all of it can evaporate in a flash when something triggers
his rage. The eye twitch is the warning that the mask is slipping. A man who has
people killed in their sleep but can't control his own anger in a room is a man
at war with himself. The court has learned to manage him, but the temper makes
him unpredictable in exactly the moments where predictability matters most.
