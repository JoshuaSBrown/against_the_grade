ILANA SARIN NOR'BALIK --- Village Girl (Tanapun)
================================================

**Status:** ⚠️ Function defined / ❌ Personality undefined

**Name:** Ilana Sarin Nor'Balik. Formally Hilana, from /h/ category
(abstract/identity). Hi-la-na, CV-CV-CV. The /h/ dropped per feminine
convention. Commoner, Tanapun local. A name that sounds young and vulnerable ---
open vowels, soft ending. Surname Sarin (warm wind) --- the same weather surname
as Baret's family, which means she's from the same sheltered side of the
mountain. She might even be a neighbor.

**Background:** A girl from Tanapun, Baret's home village. Kesal bothers her
when he visits the village in his capacity as Haldrin's retainer's son. She's
not a plot-driver --- she's a witness to Kesal's character. The reader sees
Kesal bother Ilana in Tanapun and files it away. When Kesal corners Elowen
later, the pattern is already established --- this isn't his first time.

Narrative Function
------------------

Ilana is the first data point in a pattern. Kesal targets girls where he has
authority --- in Tanapun, he has it as the retainer's child visiting the
village. The reader sees him bother Ilana and registers it as texture. When the
same behavior escalates with Reska in Pakran and then with Elowen, the reader
understands: this is who Kesal is, everywhere he goes.

The proximity to Baret's family (same surname root, same side of the mountain,
possibly a neighbor) makes Baret's inaction sharper. This isn't a stranger being
mistreated --- it's someone from his own hillside. When Baret finally intervenes
for Elowen in Chapter 6, the reader can ask why he didn't act for Ilana. Whether
it's because Elowen matters more to him or because his self-loathing finally
crossed a threshold --- either reading works, and neither is comfortable.
