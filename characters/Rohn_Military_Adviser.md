ROHN --- Military Adviser
-------------------------

**Status:** ⚠️ Personality well-sketched / ⚠️ Arc partially defined

**Name:** Rohn (language-compatible: /r/ onset --- movement/direction/paths
category. CVC structure. A commander whose name means "directed movement" is
fitting)

**Role:** Adviser to Baret in Book 2. Former soldier. Becomes someone Baret
comes to trust deeply.

**Background:** A veteran who's survived long enough to have opinions about
everything and eight children to show for it.

**What He Carries:** Vigilance as a lifestyle. He has trained his eight children
to attack him at random as part of his personal training regimen --- to stay
sharp, to never be complacent. His household is a constant low-grade ambush
scenario. Kids dropping from rafters, hiding behind doors, jumping out of
bushes. He believes surprise is almost always the key to victory, and he lives
that principle at home.

**Strength:** Wisdom earned through experience. He has a lot of practical
knowledge that helps Baret navigate the transition from rebel leader to war
commander. He sees patterns, understands logistics, knows when to push and when
to wait. His trust, once given, is solid. He gives it to Baret because Baret
acts decisively and earns it.

**Weakness:** He misjudges people who don't operate the way he does. He thinks
Darek is weak because Darek avoids confrontation, uses humor, and smooths things
over instead of taking hard positions. To a man who trains his children to
ambush him, diplomacy looks like cowardice. He's wrong about Darek, but he's
wrong in a way that's completely consistent with who he is --- and it means his
initial assessment of Darek could resurface in Book 4 when Darek appears to
betray Baret. Does the old soldier see through the manipulation, or does he
revert to "I always knew he was weak"?

**Book 2 Arc:** Baret's trusted adviser. His tension with Darek runs through the
book --- Rohn dismisses Darek as someone who doesn't know what he's doing, while
Darek is insecure and struggling to find his footing in a military context where
his social skills seem useless. Near the end of Book 2, Darek does something
that earns Rohn's respect --- not by becoming a soldier, but by proving that his
approach has value Rohn couldn't see.

**What's Missing:**

-   His relationship with his children beyond the training game --- is he a good
    father or just a demanding one?
-   Whether he survives into later books and what role he plays
-   His reaction to Darek's apparent betrayal in Book 4
-   The specific moment Darek earns his respect in Book 2
