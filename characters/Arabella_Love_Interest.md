## ARABELA --- The Betrothed

**Status:** ⚠️ Function clear / ⚠️ Personal detail missing

**Name:** Arabela (formally Harabela, from seed language /h/ onset ---
identity/social category, with feminine /h/-drop. Ha-ra-be-la, CV-CV-CV-CV. In
casual speech the /h/ drops. From /s/ category root sa-ra-bel, "quality of
directed substance" --- thematically apt for someone whose value is defined by
how she appears)

**Background:** Elevated by beauty and family connections. From the
higher-status branch of Elowen's family. Betrothed to Kullen not out of love but
as an arranged marriage --- her father traded her as a political move to secure
loyalty with Haldrin's house. She was treated as property, valued for what she
could be exchanged for rather than who she was.

**Core Arc:**

- **Book 1:** Baret falls for her. She goes to Baret when he wins, because he
  now represents power --- and more importantly, escape from the arrangement
  that defined her as currency. But Baret's world doesn't offer the security she
  needs.
- **Books 2--3:** Leaves Baret when she sees he can't give her what she wants.
  Returns to Kullen (or the power structure Kullen represents) --- not because
  she loves it, but because it's the system she was built to navigate.
- **Later books:** Has a son. The betrothed's son becomes relevant to the plot
  (details unclear).

**Personality:** Proud and indifferent --- because to care is to show weakness.
She was treated as property when she was younger, valued for her beauty and her
family connections rather than as a person. She learned that vulnerability gets
exploited, so she armored herself with detachment. She doesn't pretend not to
see the game --- she plays it openly and without apology.

**What She Carries:** A doll her father made for her. It's one of the few things
he did that showed love rather than transactional calculation, and she holds
onto it because it proves to her --- privately, where no one can see --- that
she was once loved for herself rather than for what she could be exchanged for.
The contrast between her public indifference and this private sentimentality is
the key to who she actually is underneath the armor.

**Strength:** She's never wrong about power. Every assessment she makes about
who will win, who will lose, who is dangerous --- she's right. She reads
political currents with precision. She leaves Baret not because she's shallow
but because she correctly identifies that his principled approach will make him
vulnerable. She looks for safety in strength because she learned early that
strength is the only reliable protection.

**Weakness:** Her pride makes her brittle. Indifference is armor, but armor is
heavy and it doesn't bend. She can't accept help, can't admit need, can't let
anyone close enough to see the doll. When the world shifts in ways her
power-reading can't predict --- like Kullen's manipulation, which looks like
normal politics on the surface --- she has no fallback. Her inability to care
openly means she has no real allies, only arrangements.

**Quirk:** She starts hiccupping when she gets nervous and is extremely
embarrassed by it. Her body betrays her composure --- the reader always knows
when her indifference is a performance because the hiccups give her away. It
also means every character in the room knows too, which makes her armor feel
fragile in exactly the moments she needs it most.
