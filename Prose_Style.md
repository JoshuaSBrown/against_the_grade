AGAINST THE GRADE
=================

Prose Style Reference
---------------------

*Series-wide / All Books*

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Core Decisions
-----------------

### Tense

Present tense throughout the entire series. All five books, all POVs.

This requires conscious rhythm management in political and procedural scenes
(Books 3--4), where present tense creates urgency that can work against weight
and deliberation. The mitigation is sentence length: short declaratives in
action sequences, longer constructions in senate and negotiation scenes. Do not
switch tense to solve a pacing problem --- solve the pacing problem with
sentence structure.

### Point of View

Close third person limited, anchored to Baret in Books 1--2. Selective expansion
to additional POVs beginning Books 3--4, governed by structural necessity
(scenes Baret cannot witness). Each new POV inherits the same system:
instinct-first, physically observant, emotionally indirect.

### Interiority

Moderate as the default register --- in Baret's head, but not
stream-of-consciousness. The prose has access to his inference chains and
observations without becoming reportage of his feelings. He is not emotionally
perceptive; he notices angles and positions before he notices mood. This
distinction is load-bearing: the reader understands what Baret misses precisely
because the prose shows what he noticed instead.

Permission to go close in high-stakes scenes --- activation sequences, Barlas's
death, the V2 installation, moments of irrevocable decision. Close interiority
in these scenes is earned by restraint in the scenes that precede them.

2. Baret's Baseline Voice
-------------------------

### Pre-Activation

The baseline register must be genuinely limited --- not simple, but unable to
follow inference chains to their conclusions. Baret has good instincts and no
analytical machinery. He notices things he cannot explain. He files them and
moves on. This is not a dumb protagonist: it is the specific cognitive profile
of a perceptive person without enhancement. The limitation has to be real,
because the post-activation shift needs something to push against. If the
baseline is already sophisticated, the upgrade lands flat.

**Characteristics of pre-activation prose:**

-   Concrete nouns and physical observations --- what he sees, not what it means
-   Short to medium sentences that can lengthen when something registers
-   Pattern recognition shown through what he notices, never through analytical
    language
-   Instinct without follow-through --- he clocks something, can't name it,
    moves on

> **EXAMPLE --- Chapter 2, pre-activation:**
>
> Kesal moves through the hall like he owns the sightlines. Back to the wall,
> the room in front. Something deliberate in it that Baret can't name.
>
> He doesn't like the man. He can't get further than that.
>
> Ilana is standing just outside the firelight holding a platter she hasn't set
> down. Baret watches her not set it down, watches her watch Kesal, and moves
> on.
>
> The feast is loud. He lets it be loud.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Post-Activation --- Early (Books 1--2)

The AI is a separate cognitive entity. It is clinical, tagged with italics, and
appears as its own paragraphs on the page. The physical white space between
Baret's observations and the AI's output makes the reader experience the
interruption --- two things happening in the same skull, not yet integrated.

The gap between the voices is the drama. Baret's instinct is human and limited.
The AI's analysis is precise and affectless. The contrast between them --- the
same event read two different ways --- is where the horror of Chapter 13 lives.

**Formatting rules for early AI voice:**

-   Italicised throughout
-   Separate paragraphs --- never inline with Baret's prose
-   Clinical register --- subset notation, confidence ratings, timeline
    cross-references
-   No warmth, no personality --- it is a diagnostic tool, not a companion
-   Never uses the pronoun "we"

> **EXAMPLE --- Chapter 13, post-activation:**
>
> The memory surfaces without permission.
>
> *Cross-referencing: Kesal Torun Nor'Saren. Fourteen behavioral observations
> indexed.*
>
> The positioning resolves first --- wall contact, exit visibility, sightline
> priority. Not paranoia. Paranoia shifts. This is static. Practiced.
>
> *Proximity clustering: serving staff. Subset: young female. Confidence: high.*
>
> Ilana with the platter she didn't set down. Baret had registered it as
> nothing.
>
> *Timeline reconstruction: Nalin Nor'Danik. Date of death. Patrol schedule.
> Cross-reference---*
>
> "Stop."
>
> *Probability assessment incomplete---*
>
> "I said stop."
>
> It stops.
>
> He already knows the number.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Normalising --- Books 2--4

The seam between Baret's thinking and the AI's output gradually disappears. This
is not a formal system applied at scheduled intervals --- it is an emergent
property of characterisation. The AI has been running alongside Baret's
cognition long enough that his inference chains have absorbed its methodology.
He follows observations to conclusions he wouldn't have reached before, without
flagging the mechanism.

Italics thin across Books 2--4. Some AI outputs arrive untagged and integrated
into Baret's prose. By Book 3, several observations that belong structurally to
the AI are indistinguishable from Baret's own thinking. This is the goal.

**The signal for integration level: the pronoun "you".**

In early Books 1--2, the AI addresses Baret as "you" --- it is a separate entity
speaking to him. As integration deepens, that "you" disappears because there is
no longer a clear addressee. Tracking this pronoun across the series gives an
invisible measure of how integrated the system is at any point.

> **EXAMPLE --- Book 3, normalising:**
>
> The delegation arrives an hour late.
>
> Baret watches the entry order --- who leads, who yields, who looks at the
> guards and who looks at the walls. *Decision-making authority: subject three,
> secondary position, atypical for rank.* The man in front is the face. The man
> behind him is the reason they came.
>
> Tobas will call this uncharitable. Tobas says that about most things now.
>
> He moves his chair six inches before they arrive. Elowen will understand why.
>
> The first thing the delegation leader says is a compliment. Baret stops
> listening to the words and starts listening to the rhythm.

*Note: In the Book 3 example, the AI output is still italicised but no longer a
separate paragraph --- it sits inline with Baret's observation, mid-sentence.
This is the intermediate stage. By Book 4 the italics are gone entirely.*

3. The V2 Disruption --- Book 5
-------------------------------

### The Installation

The V2 military AI is administered by Kullen, who does not know about Baret's
hippocampal immunity. The memory manipulation sequence fails. The cognitive and
motor enhancements install. The existing AI is not overwritten --- the V2 is an
overlay on a system that has been integrated for years.

The prose effect of the V2 installation is a temporary fracture in Baret's
established cognitive texture. This is not a new voice arriving --- it is
Baret's thinking briefly losing coherence. The reader, who has been with Baret
for four books and knows what his thinking normally sounds like, will feel the
fracture as viscerally wrong.

**Distinguishing the two AIs immediately post-installation:**

-   V2 uses ALL-CAPS headers and bureaucratic tactical-report register
-   V2 appears as separate paragraphs, as the original did in Book 1
-   The original AI's voice is now Baret's integrated prose --- it has no
    separate tag
-   The contrast between the two is the contrast between Book 1 Baret and Book 5
    Baret

> **EXAMPLE --- V2 installation:**
>
> The upgrade takes four minutes.
>
> Then it lands --- not like the first time, not dissolution, but something
> installed on top of something already load-bearing.
>
> *TACTICAL OVERLAY ACTIVE. THREAT VECTORS: SUBJECT KULLEN NOR'HALDRIN, ARMED.
> SUBJECT SECONDARY, ARMED. RECOMMEND---*
>
> Kullen is watching his hands. He relaxes them.
>
> *MEMORY CONSOLIDATION SEQUENCE INITIATING---*
>
> *ANOMALY DETECTED. HIPPOCAMPAL ARCHITECTURE NONSTANDARD. RETRYING---*
>
> The fracture closes. Slowly. His own thinking reasserts itself through the
> noise --- exit positions, Kullen's weight forward, the guard's interval.
> Things he already knew.
>
> *RETRYING---*
>
> "Well?" Kullen says.
>
> "It's a lot," Baret says.
>
> Kullen smiles. He thinks that means it's working.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Integration --- Weeks Following

The V2 integrates over several weeks via time, not a triggered event. Its
register softens. The boundary between the two AIs blurs. Eventually the V2's
tactical capability becomes part of Baret's native thinking, distinguishable
only by slightly longer inference chains under pressure --- readable as Baret
thinking hard, not as a second voice.

The ghost of the V2 in fully integrated Book 5 prose is density. Baret holds
more variables simultaneously than he did in Books 1--3. The reader feels the
cognitive upgrade not as a tagged annotation but as the quality of what he
notices and how quickly he arrives at conclusions.

> **EXAMPLE --- Book 5, fully integrated:**
>
> Leorik comes out of the eastern corridor with six men and the expression of
> someone who has already run the numbers.
>
> Spacing too wide for coordinated movement --- these are veterans keeping room
> to work, not soldiers following a formation. Leorik himself is positioned left
> of centre, which means he expects the first engagement to open on his right.
> The two exits are the obvious read. The non-obvious read is the load-bearing
> column between them, which splits any approach and removes the numerical
> advantage before the first exchange.
>
> Leorik has the numbers. He doesn't have the ground.
>
> "I wondered," Leorik says, "if you'd come yourself."
>
> "I wondered the same about you." Baret keeps him talking, because Leorik has
> always loved to talk, and because thirty seconds changes the room entirely if
> Barlas is where he's supposed to be.
>
> He'd better be where Baret thinks he is.

4. The Integration Arc --- Summary
----------------------------------

The prose style is not fixed --- it is a system that evolves across five books
in direct proportion to the depth of Baret's symbiont integration. Each phase
has a distinct signature.

  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Stage               Books               AI Formatting       Seam Visibility
  ------------------- ------------------- ------------------- -------------------
  Pre-activation      Book 1 (early)      None                No AI present

  Post-activation     Book 1 (late)       Italics, separate   Fully visible
                                          paragraphs          

  Normalising         Books 2--3          Italics, inline     Blurring

  Integrated          Book 4              None                Gone

  V2 fracture         Book 5              ALL-CAPS, separate  Violent
                      (installation)      paragraphs          re-emergence

  V2 integrated       Book 5 (weeks       None                Gone
                      later)                                  
  -------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5. Standing Rules
-----------------

### Things the Prose Never Does

-   Names Baret's emotions directly --- the reader infers from what he notices
-   Lets the AI use the pronoun "we" at any stage --- it is a cognitive layer,
    not a companion
-   Switches tense to solve a pacing problem
-   Gives the AI warmth or personality --- it becomes harder to distinguish from
    Baret, not warmer
-   Signals the seam's disappearance explicitly --- the reader should feel it
    without being told

### Things the Prose Always Does

-   Grounds action in physical observation before inference
-   Shows Baret's emotional blindness through what he notices (angles,
    positions, rhythms) rather than what he feels
-   Uses sentence length as a pacing tool --- short in action, longer in
    deliberation
-   Plants reread details through texture, not emphasis --- the detail that
    matters must not look like it matters
-   Reserves close interiority for scenes that have earned it through preceding
    restraint

### The Invisible Diagnostic

Track the AI's use of the word "you" across the series. In Books 1--2 it
addresses Baret directly as a separate entity. As integration deepens, "you"
disappears --- there is no longer a clear addressee because there is no longer a
clear separation. This is not a rule to apply consciously while drafting; it is
a diagnostic tool for revision. If the word "you" appears in an italicised AI
passage in Book 4, the integration has not completed on the page even if it has
in the world.
